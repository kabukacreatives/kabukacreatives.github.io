The Consistency Trap — You have been chasing the wrong goal entirely.

Subtopic 1 of 3
"Just be consistent" is the worst fitness advice ever given
Educational
Hook
Consistency is not a personality trait. Stop trying to grow one.
Core Insight
Telling someone to "just be consistent" is like telling someone to "just be rich." Consistency is an outcome of a well-designed system, not a character virtue. When women fail to stay consistent, they blame themselves — when they should blame the advice. The gym industry profits from your self-blame because it sends you back to buy another membership.
3 Do This Today Steps
  • Audit your routine: which days consistently get skipped? That is a design flaw, not a discipline flaw.
  • Replace "I need to be more consistent" with "What one thing can I remove to make showing up easier?"
  • Set a minimum viable workout (15 min) for hard weeks so skipping becomes the harder choice.

Subtopic 3 of 3
The 2-minute rule: the smallest possible start is the most powerful
Educational
Hook
The hardest part is not the workout. It is walking through the door.
Core Insight
Behavioral science shows that starting an action — even in a reduced form — dramatically increases the probability of completing it. A bad workout you showed up to beats a perfect workout you skipped by every measurable metric. Committing to just showing up removes performance pressure and replaces it with identity reinforcement.
3 Do This Today Steps
  • On your lowest-energy days, commit only to arriving — not performing. Once you are there, you will almost always do more.
  • Replace "I will go when I feel ready" with "I go, then I feel ready." Action creates motivation, not the other way around.
  • Track attendance only for 30 days — not weight, not reps. Just days attended. Build the identity before the results.
